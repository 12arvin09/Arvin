In this project, I built a smart backpack designed to help visually impaired people with real-time location sensing, time-telling, and sharing their live location with others. The idea came from how fast cities like Ulaanbaatar are changing, which makes it harder for blind people to travel independently.. So I tried to create something simple, wearable, and helpful. The backpack uses a GPS and speaker setup. By pressing a button, the user can hear their current time or location. Another button sends their location via SMS to a saved contact. I used the Air780EG board because it already includes GPS and cellular features in a compact size. All the components were placed in a small 3D-printed case, clipped onto a regular backpack strap for easy use. Testing showed that the time and SMS features worked really well, but location accuracy sometimes dropped when the GPS wasn’t updating quickly or when used indoors. It also needed 2–3 minutes to fully start, and walking movements made the updates slower. To read more about my results, please check the documentation below.. To make the device more convenient, I had to research batteries and choose the right type. I also worked on optimizing the LuatOS asynchronous code. Since mid-February, I’ve been working on the second edition. It hasn’t reached the version I want yet, but soon I’ll test it with users other than myself. Still, I’m happy this project is ongoing, with challenges that are tough . . . But definitely solvable..
