Projects

Spelling Letters Through Brainwaves

This paper explores the potential of brain-computer interfaces (BCIs), a key area within human-computer interaction (HCI), using non-invasive EEG technology to detect letters from brain signals. While BCIs are rarely studied in Mongolia, this project marks an early effort to bridge brain signals with technology, laying the foundation for future development in the field.

Smart Cane For Visually Impaired People

This paper presents the first version of a smart cane designed to support visually impaired individuals, addressing key challenges such as water, obstacle, and hole detection. Using five infrared sensors and a water sensor controlled by a microcontroller, the cane identifies various obstacles and provides real-time audio feedback. Developed in response to limited infrastructure and slow IoT progress, this project marks an early step toward improving accessibility for visually impaired users.

Location Sensing Smart Backpack

This paper introduces a smart backpack designed to support visually impaired individuals as they navigate changing urban environments. Equipped with features like location sensing, time telling, and location sharing, the device enhances safety, independence, and daily functionality. It represents an early step toward long-term, inclusive solutions that empower users and adapt to evolving urban challenges.
