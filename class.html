Biggest Takeaway : Failing fast taught me more than succeeding quickly.

People often see class presidents as leaders, bridges, or guides. But I always saw myself more like a processor (hahaha): quietly taking in everyone’s input, holding their trust, sorting through issues, and making sure nothing got lost or ignored. Not flashy, but dependable. Not the loudest, but often the most trusted. My journey began on September 1st, 2018, when my teacher chose me to be class president. I was scared. It was my first time leading, while everyone else had at least tried it for a semester before. But even through the fear, I took on the role. The following year, I was chosen again not by a teacher this time, but by my classmates. That’s when it truly felt like my presidency had started. I wasn’t the loud, guiding, spotlighted kind of leader. I was just the one who always stood in between trusted by both teachers and students. During the quarantine lessons, some classmates (especially the boys!) wouldn’t even set their alarms. Instead, they’d say the night before, “Arvin, please wake me up tomorrow.” And of course, I would calling 10 to 20 people every morning, one by one, just to make sure everyone showed up. That kind of responsibility wasn’t just about schoolwork and it was about care. Over the past five years, we went through a lot together. Which means, as class president, I went through a lot too( from talent shows and sports championships to Teacher’s Day, Women’s Day, Mongolian Soldier’s Day, and more happening annually) These amazing years didn’t just teach me how to be a leader. They taught me what it really means to be a good person : not by being perfect, but by being dependable, caring, and real.. And for that, I’ll always be grateful.
