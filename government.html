Biggest Takeaway : Failing fast taught me more than succeeding quickly.

Since 9th grade, during the 2020 quarantine, I still clearly remember how my eyes were glued to the computer screen : watching YouTube videos of university professors teaching with such clarity on their digital boards. Our school was closed, and the only way I could dive deeper into the subjects I loved was by searching, reading, and learning online. Looking back, I’m truly grateful for those days and especially for those kind teachers who uploaded their lessons for free. They had no idea, but they helped shape who I am today. A couple of years later, during a government-led education project, I was lucky enough to stand where those teachers once stood. In 11th grade, I recorded my first official teaching video. I prepared my speech without pauses, wanting to make it smooth and clear. I revised everything I knew, from the very beginning, and for the first time, I felt part of something much bigger : a nationwide effort to reach students across Mongolia. I don’t know how many rural students saw my video, but I really hope it helped even a few of them. I’m deeply thankful to the national university professors and my beloved physics teacher who gave me the chance to stand behind that glowing lightboard and connect with students far away.
